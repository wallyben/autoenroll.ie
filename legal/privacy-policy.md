# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date**: November 23, 2025  
**Last Updated**: November 23, 2025

## 1. Introduction

AutoEnroll.ie ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our pension auto-enrolment compliance service.

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Account Information
- Email address
- Password (encrypted)
- Account creation date

### 2.2 Employee Data (Temporarily Processed)
When you upload payroll data, we temporarily process:
- Employee names
- Dates of birth
- PPS numbers (if provided)
- Employment dates
- Salary information
- Email addresses (if provided)

**IMPORTANT**: Employee data is NEVER permanently stored. It is processed in memory and deleted immediately after your session completes.

### 2.3 Usage Data
- Upload timestamps
- Number of records processed
- Feature usage statistics (anonymized)
- Error logs (without personal data)

### 2.4 Payment Information
- Processed securely by Stripe
- We do not store credit card details
- Only subscription status is retained

## 3. How We Use Your Information

### 3.1 Service Provision
- Validate employee data
- Calculate auto-enrolment eligibility
- Compute pension contributions
- Generate compliance reports

### 3.2 Account Management
- Authenticate users
- Manage subscriptions
- Provide customer support

### 3.3 Service Improvement
- Analyze usage patterns (anonymized)
- Improve validation accuracy
- Enhance user experience

## 4. Data Retention

### 4.1 Zero Retention for Employee Data
- Upload files deleted within minutes
- Processing results cleared from memory
- No permanent storage of employee PII

### 4.2 Account Data
- Retained while account is active
- Deleted within 30 days of account deletion

### 4.3 Usage Metadata
- Retained for 30 days for analytics
- Anonymized aggregates retained indefinitely

## 5. Data Security

### 5.1 Technical Measures
- HTTPS/TLS encryption for data in transit
- Bcrypt password hashing
- Pseudonymisation of personal data during processing
- Regular security audits
- Access controls and authentication

### 5.2 Organizational Measures
- Staff training on data protection
- Confidentiality agreements
- Incident response procedures
- Regular security reviews

## 6. Data Sharing

### 6.1 We Do Not Sell Your Data
We will never sell your personal information to third parties.

### 6.2 Service Providers
We may share data with:
- **Stripe**: Payment processing (no employee data)
- **Hosting providers**: Infrastructure (encrypted data)
- **Email service**: Transactional emails (no employee data)

### 6.3 Legal Requirements
We may disclose data if required by law or to:
- Comply with legal obligations
- Protect our rights and safety
- Investigate fraud or security issues

## 7. Your Rights (GDPR)

### 7.1 Right to Access
Request a copy of your account data.

### 7.2 Right to Rectification
Correct inaccurate account information.

### 7.3 Right to Erasure
Delete your account and all associated data.

### 7.4 Right to Data Portability
Export your usage data in machine-readable format.

### 7.5 Right to Object
Object to processing of your data for certain purposes.

### 7.6 Right to Withdraw Consent
Withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior processing.

## 8. Cookies and Tracking

### 8.1 Essential Cookies
- Authentication tokens
- Session management
- Security features

### 8.2 Analytics Cookies
- Anonymous usage statistics
- Can be disabled in browser settings

We do not use advertising or tracking cookies.

## 9. Children's Privacy

Our service is not intended for individuals under 18. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

## 10. International Data Transfers

- All data processing occurs within the EU
- Standard Contractual Clauses used where necessary
- Adequate safeguards in place for all transfers

## 11. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach:
- We will notify affected users within 72 hours
- Details of the breach and mitigation steps provided
- Regulatory authorities notified as required

## 12. Changes to This Policy

- We may update this policy periodically
- Users notified of material changes
- Continued use constitutes acceptance

## 15. Your Responsibilities

As a data controller uploading employee data:
- Ensure you have legal basis to process employee data
- Inform employees of data processing
- Obtain necessary consents
- Comply with applicable data protection laws

## 16. Automated Decision Making

We do not use automated decision making or profiling that produces legal effects.

## 17. Pseudonymisation Details

Employee data is pseudonymised using:
- SHA-256 hashing for identifiers
- Name redaction in reports
- Date rounding (year only)
- Salary banding for analytics
